Sundar Pichai is an Indian-American business executive and Google Inc. Chief Executive Officer (CEO) He was born on July 12, 1972 in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, Tamil Brahman. His father, Regunith Pichai, used to work as a senior electric engineer in General Electric and managed a factory that made components of electricity and his mother was a stenographer before his birth. He belonged to a middle-class family.He completed his school education from Chennai and earned his bachelor's degree in metallurgical engineering from Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur. After graduation, he followed the MIB from Stanford University of Medical Sciences and the MBA in Engineering and MBA from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania.

His name was respectively named after the Sibel scholar and Palmer Scholar and the Wharton School at Stanford University. After completing MBA in 2002, he planned to pursue a PhD from Stanford University, but went out of Applied materials as an engineer and product manager for a brief period and joined it. He went with McKensey & Co. to work with a management consultant In April 2004, he was interviewed and initially chosen by Google, he worked on Google's search toolbar, which would allow users of Internet Explorer and Firefox to have easy access to Google search engines. In 2006 Microsoft was working hard on Google's search toolbar while changing its default search engine for 'Bing' from 'Google'. However, this was not easy, but Pichai succeeded in convincing computer makers so that Google Toolbar could be pre-installed, reducing the impact of Microsoft's inherent changes.
